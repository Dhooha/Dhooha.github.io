Research Statement

  • Viruses are the most abundant and diverse organisms on earth, unfortunately we do not know much about them: the number of viruses is estimated to be more than 10^30, whereas we know only 700K viral genomes to this date. For this reason, we need computational tools to predict the function of the new viral proteins. The current tools can be divided into 2 methods: alignment-based and composition-based methods. The alignment-based methods present few limitations because they are slow. On the other hand, the composition-based methods are less investigated; we combine composition-based methods with machine learning to predict the function of viral protein sequences.
